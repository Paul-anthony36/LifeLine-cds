Sukkur TB-Clinic

Lifeline CDS Established a medical branch with priority on Tb treatment since 1995. This is performed mobile clinics in remote rural areas of upper Sindh where there is less access to medical treatment. At the same time a clinic in Sukkur town was established. There patients are seen on two days a week and Tb-patients who are detected in villages come for checkup and initiation of treatment. Afterwards they are mainly followed up in the village for continuation of treatment which usually goes on for 6 to 9 months. In the beginning Lifeline used to provide all the medicines even in cases of multi-resistant Tb except small self-contributions for the purpose of compliance. Now Lifeline works in cooperation with WHO and the national Tb program through EDO Sukkur in 2009. Since then the Tb medicines are provided by WHO. Lifeline follows the treatment guidelines of WHO since that time. Lifeline bears all the expenses regarding operation of the clinic, two vehicles, staff etc. Due to medical services of Lifeline many of people have been treated regardless of creed or ethnic origin. Hundreds of people have been healed from Tb, many families have been saved from poverty as family head/supporter has been treated and healed.
